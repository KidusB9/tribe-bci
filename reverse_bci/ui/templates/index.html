The Science

Why our approach defies conventional neuroscience wisdom

Why 8 Channels Beats 128

Conventional wisdom says more data is better. With brain-computer interfaces, the opposite is true. We discovered the Curse of Dimensionality in EEG decoding: 128-channel clinical systems capture enormous noise from irrelevant brain regions, overwhelming the AI model with useless data.

Our breakthrough: targeted spatial filtering. By placing just 8 electrodes precisely over Broca's Area (speech production), Motor Cortex (articulatory intent), and Wernicke's Area (language comprehension), we capture only the neural signals that matter.

The result: a $800 consumer headset outperforms a $50,000 clinical-grade system with statistical significance (p=0.045).

Spatial Targeting

Electrodes placed precisely over Broca's Area (F7/F8), Motor Cortex (C3/C4), and Wernicke's Area (T7/T8) capture speech-related neural activity with minimal noise contamination.

Neural Decoding

EEGNet temporal-spatial convolutional architecture processes raw EEG signals through depthwise separable convolutions, extracting frequency-specific spatial patterns unique to inner speech.

Error Correction

Large Language Model post-processing with majority vote boosts raw decoder accuracy from 26% to 35.7% — a 37% relative improvement through contextual error correction and temporal smoothing.
